First Aid & Life Saving Tips
These tips are not a substitute for professional medical training. Consider taking a certified first aid course.

Basic CPR Steps

1. Check for Responsiveness

Tap the person's shoulder and shout "Are you okay?" If no response, call for emergency help immediately.

2. Open the Airway

Place the person on their back. Tilt their head back slightly by lifting the chin to open the airway.

3. Check for Breathing

Look, listen, and feel for no more than 10 seconds. If the person isn't breathing, begin CPR.

4. Chest Compressions

Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest. Place your other hand on top and interlock fingers. Press down hard and fast - about 2 inches deep at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.

5. Rescue Breaths (if trained)

After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths. Pinch the nose shut, make a complete seal over the person's mouth, and blow in for about 1 second to make the chest rise.

6. Continue Until Help Arrives

Continue cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until the person starts breathing or emergency responders take over.

Other Important First Aid Tips

Severe Bleeding

Apply direct pressure on the wound with a clean cloth until bleeding stops. Don't remove soaked cloths - add more on top.

Burns

Cool the burn under cool (not cold) running water for 10-15 minutes. Don't apply ice, butter, or ointments.

Choking

For adults: Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver). For infants: Use back blows and chest thrusts.
